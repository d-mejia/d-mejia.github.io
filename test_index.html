Biography:

Hello! I am currently a Research Assistant/Study Coordinator at the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center, working for Jullie W. Pan, MD/PhD and Hoby Hetherington, PhD in Neurology and Radiology. I assist in 3T and 7T neuroimaging studies on veterans with mTBI and/or PTSD, epilepsy patients, neuro-oncology patients, and healthy controls. These studies use an imaging technique known as magnetic resonance spectroscopy to study the metabolic processes in the brain.


In the fall of 2015, I plan on applying to graduate programs with a focus on Quantitative Methods/Psychometrics.


Some more background: I completed my B.S. in Psychology (Behavioral and Cognitive Neuroscience concentration) with minors in Statistics and Literature at the University of Florida. As an undergraduate, I volunteered as a research assistant in three psychology labs (one with a clinical focus, one with a behavioral focus, and one with a developmental focus). I also participated in an independent group research project in a Sensory Processing lab under the supervision of Andreas Keil, PhD. We created a testing paradigm to see if biased feedback affected working memory. The paper is currently under review for publication.
