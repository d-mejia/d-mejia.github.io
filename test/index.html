Biography:

Hello! My name is Daniela. I am currently a Research Assistant at the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center, working for Jullie W. Pan, MD/PhD and Hoby Hetherington, PhD in Neurology and Radiology. I coordinate and assist with 3T and 7T neuroimaging studies on veterans with mTBI and/or PTSD, epilepsy patients, neuro-oncology patients, and healthy controls. These studies use an imaging technique known as magnetic resonance spectroscopy to study the metabolic processes in the brain. Outside of work, I am also taking night classes at the University of Pittsburgh in math and computer science.


In the fall of 2016, I plan on applying to graduate programs with a focus on Quantitative Psychology and/or Psychometrics.


Some more background: I completed my B.S. in Psychology (Behavioral and Cognitive Neuroscience) with minors in Statistics and Literature at the University of Florida. As an undergraduate, I volunteered as a research assistant in three psychology labs. I also participated in a group research project in a Sensory Processing lab under the supervision of Andreas Keil, PhD. We created a testing paradigm to see if biased feedback affected working memory on a novel working memory task, which I created using PsychoPy.


Even more background: I am Colombian American, and I know a lot about my ancestry from taking a commerical DNA test. For example, I know 12% of my ancestry is Indigenous, ~47% is Spanish, 2% is Scandanavian, etc... (see the Interests page for more info).
