Comet


I learned the other day that in Spanish kites are called comets. Comets are also called comets. It’s the same word: cometa. I love the imagery of that— of a little kid going to the beach, unraveling with string, and flying his own comet like a dog on a leash. I went to a planetarium show once where they said the star of Bethlehem that guided the magi to Jesus probably wasn’t a real star. They don’t know for sure what it was but it might’ve been a conjunction, which is when the path of two planets gets really, really close and the light that produces looks as bright as a star in the sky— and so that’s what they called it. I love the imagery of that as well and find it very relatable. For how many times have I looked out at night searching for a sign from the universe, desperate for a sign from God?

--Dani
